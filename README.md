# Ola-Navigation

Ola stands among India’s top ride-hailing services, providing an extensive range of mobility options such as cabs, auto-rickshaws, and bike rides. Established in 2010, it transformed urban transportation by using technology to seamlessly link riders with available drivers. Ola’s offerings go beyond regular point-to-point rides, covering rental vehicles and intercity travel, making it a versatile and reliable platform for different commuting and travel requirements.

The Power BI report for Ola summarizes essential performance insights and operational trends for the period from July 1, 2024, to July 30, 2024. During this time, Ola recorded 20.41K bookings with a total booking value of ₹2M. The dashboard shows that 62% of the rides were successfully completed, while the remaining cancellations occurred due to factors such as “Driver Not Found” (9.9%), “Canceled by Driver” (10.2%), and “Canceled by Customer” (17.91%).

Payment Analysis indicates a strong inclination toward cash transactions, amounting to ₹3.8M. UPI payments followed at ₹2.8M, whereas credit and debit card payments contributed only a small share, reflecting relatively low adoption of card-based methods.

Customer Insights: The five highest-value customers generated a collective booking value of ₹17,010, demonstrating strong engagement from repeat users. Customer cancellations were primarily driven by reasons like “Driver asked to cancel” (29.31%) and “Change of plans” (19.61%). On the other hand, drivers attributed cancellations to “Personal and vehicle issues” (34.56%) and “Customer-related issues” (29.12%).

Ride Trends: Overall booking activity stayed steady, with noticeable surges on a few dates. Daily ride distances showed consistent patterns with minor fluctuations, and average driver and customer ratings hovered around 4.0 stars, indicating generally positive service quality.
